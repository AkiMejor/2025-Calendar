Easter Sunday

Easter Sunday is the most important and joyful celebration in the Christian liturgical calendar, commemorating the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead, as described in the New Testament. Christians believe that Jesus' resurrection on the third day after His crucifixion conquered death and sin, offering eternal life to all who believe in Him. Easter marks the culmination of the Holy Week observances, following Good Friday and Black Saturday, and signifies the triumph of life over death, light over darkness, and hope over despair.

In the Philippines, Easter Sunday is a day of great celebration for Christians, especially Catholics, who make up the majority of the population. It is a time for joy, as Filipinos commemorate Christ’s victory over death with Masses, prayers, and family gatherings. The day is filled with vibrant and festive traditions, marking the end of the solemn days of Holy Week and the beginning of the Easter season, which lasts for 50 days until Pentecost.

What Do People Do?

On Easter Sunday, Filipinos celebrate the resurrection of Jesus with great joy and enthusiasm. One of the most popular traditions is the Salubong, a pre-dawn ritual in which communities gather to reenact the meeting between the risen Christ and His mother, the Virgin Mary. During this event, a statue of the Virgin Mary is covered in a black veil, which is ceremoniously removed as she "meets" the risen Christ, symbolizing the joy and hope of the resurrection. The Salubong is often followed by Mass, where Filipinos participate in Eucharistic celebrations to rejoice in the victory of Christ over death.
