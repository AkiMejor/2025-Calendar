Maundy Thursday

Maundy Thursday, known as Holy Thursday in many Christian traditions, is observed as part of the Holy Week leading up to Easter. It commemorates the Last Supper of Jesus Christ with His disciples before His crucifixion, as recorded in the New Testament. The term "Maundy" comes from the Latin word "mandatum," meaning "command," referencing Jesus’ command to His disciples to love one another as He has loved them (John 13:34). It is a day of reflection, humility, and service, highlighting Christ's act of washing His disciples' feet as a symbol of servant leadership.

In the Philippines, a predominantly Christian country, Maundy Thursday is a solemn and significant day observed by Catholics and other Christian denominations. The holiday marks the beginning of the Easter Triduum, which includes Good Friday and Easter Sunday. It is a time for remembering Christ’s sacrifice and His teachings of love, humility, and compassion. In the Catholic tradition, it is also the day for the Mass of the Lord's Supper, which often includes the ritual of foot washing.

What Do People Do?

On Maundy Thursday, many Filipino Catholics attend church services for the Misa de la Cena del Señor (Mass of the Lord's Supper), which is often held in the evening. This mass includes the reenactment of Jesus washing the feet of His disciples, symbolizing humility and service. After the mass, some Filipinos participate in the tradition of Visita Iglesia, where they visit several churches to pray and reflect on the Passion of Christ. Maundy Thursday is a day of fasting and penance, with many observing quiet and reflective activities as they prepare for the solemnity of Good Friday. In some communities, processions, re-enactments of the Last Supper, and other religious activities are held to deepen the spiritual significance of the day.
