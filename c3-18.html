Good Friday

Good Friday is one of the most solemn days in Christianity, observed annually during Holy Week, which falls two days before Easter Sunday. It commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ and His death at Calvary, as described in the New Testament. This day marks the culmination of Christ’s Passion, where Christians reflect on His suffering, sacrifice, and the ultimate act of love for humanity. The term "Good" in Good Friday may seem paradoxical, as it remembers a tragic event, but it signifies the profound significance of Christ's sacrifice for the redemption of mankind and the opening of salvation through His death.

In the Philippines, Good Friday is a highly significant observance, especially for Catholics and many other Christian denominations. It is considered a day of mourning and reflection on the sacrifice that Jesus made for humanity. The day is marked by church services, processions, and other religious observances, emphasizing themes of suffering, redemption, and hope. Filipino traditions, such as the Pabasa (a chanting of the Passion of Christ) and penitential acts, are common during Good Friday, as many people seek to spiritually connect with the pain and suffering Christ endured.

What Do People Do?

On Good Friday, Filipinos observe a day of solemnity and reflection, with many attending church services to commemorate the death of Jesus Christ. The Good Friday liturgy often includes the Veneration of the Cross, where believers approach and venerate the cross, symbolizing reverence for Christ's sacrifice. Many Filipinos engage in the practice of Visita Iglesia (church visits), where they pray and reflect on Christ's Passion at different churches.

In some parts of the country, penitents reenact the Passion of Christ, participating in processions and flagellations as acts of devotion and penance. The "Siete Palabras" (Seven Last Words) service, reflecting on Christ’s final words on the cross, is also a common tradition. The day is a time for fasting, prayer, and self-reflection as people remember the suffering of Christ and the significance of His sacrifice for the salvation of mankind. The solemnity of Good Friday sets the tone for the joyous celebration of Easter, when Christians believe Jesus was resurrected.
