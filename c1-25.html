People Power Anniversary

The People Power Anniversary, celebrated annually on February 25 in the Philippines, commemorates the historic 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution. This peaceful uprising marked the end of Ferdinand Marcos’ two-decade authoritarian rule and the restoration of democracy in the country. Sparked by widespread public discontent over corruption, human rights abuses, and electoral fraud, millions of Filipinos from all walks of life gathered along Epifanio de los Santos Avenue (EDSA) in Metro Manila. With prayers, songs, and unity, the movement demonstrated the power of nonviolent resistance, eventually leading to the ouster of Marcos and the ascent of Corazon Aquino as the nation’s new leader.

The EDSA Revolution became an international symbol of democracy and nonviolent protest, inspiring similar movements around the world. The celebration of the People Power Anniversary serves as a reminder of the Filipino people's collective strength and determination in reclaiming their freedom. It highlights the importance of civic engagement, solidarity, and vigilance in protecting democracy for future generations.

What Do People Do?

On the People Power Anniversary, Filipinos commemorate the historic event through various activities that honor the spirit of unity and democracy. Ceremonies and programs are held at the People Power Monument and other significant sites, featuring speeches by government leaders, testimonials from revolution participants, and cultural performances. Educational institutions, museums, and media outlets reflect on the events of 1986, offering historical retrospectives to inspire younger generations. Many Filipinos also use the day to advocate for democracy, justice, and human rights, drawing parallels to current social issues. The anniversary serves as both a celebration of past achievements and a call to remain vigilant in safeguarding the nation’s democratic ideals.
