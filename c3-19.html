Black Saturday

Black Saturday, also known as Holy Saturday, is the day following Good Friday and the final day of the Holy Week observance in Christianity. It commemorates the day that Jesus Christ's body lay in the tomb after His crucifixion, before His resurrection on Easter Sunday. It is a day of mourning, reflection, and silence, as Christians recall the darkness and grief felt by Christ’s followers following His death. For many, Black Saturday is a day of waiting and anticipation, as the faithful prepare for the joyful celebration of the resurrection of Jesus on Easter Sunday.

In the Philippines, Black Saturday is observed with solemnity and reverence, although it is not as ritualistically active as Good Friday. It is a day for quiet reflection, prayer, and preparing spiritually for the Easter Vigil, the evening service on Saturday night that marks the start of Easter Sunday. The tradition emphasizes the silence of the tomb and the sense of waiting for the triumph of life over death. It is a significant day in the Christian calendar, representing the period of Jesus' burial before His miraculous resurrection.

What Do People Do?

On Black Saturday, many Filipinos observe a day of quiet reflection, prayer, and penance. Church services are typically subdued, with a focus on contemplating Christ's death and the silence of the tomb. The Easter Vigil mass, which begins in the evening, is a central tradition, marking the transition from mourning to the celebration of Christ’s resurrection. This mass often includes the lighting of the Paschal candle, symbolizing Christ as the Light of the World, and the reading of the salvation history from creation to Christ’s resurrection.
