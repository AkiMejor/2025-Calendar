Start of Ramadan

The start of Ramadan is a significant event for Muslims in the Philippines, marking the beginning of a month-long period of fasting, prayer, and reflection. Ramadan is the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar and is considered the holiest month in Islam. Its exact start date depends on the sighting of the crescent moon, making it slightly different each year. The month commemorates the first revelation of the Quran to the Prophet Muhammad, a pivotal moment in Islamic faith.

In the Philippines, where Islam is the second-largest religion, Ramadan holds deep cultural and spiritual importance, especially among Muslim communities in Mindanao and other regions. It is a time for spiritual renewal, self-discipline, and community bonding. Filipino Muslims observe the practice of fasting from dawn to sunset, abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs, while focusing on prayer, charity, and acts of kindness.

What Do People Do?

At the start of Ramadan, Filipino Muslims prepare for a month of fasting and worship by gathering with family and community members to welcome this sacred period. Special prayers, known as Tarawih, are performed in mosques after the evening Isha prayer. Families stock up on food for suhoor (pre-dawn meal) and iftar (meal to break the fast at sunset), often preparing traditional dishes like pastil, piyanggang, and other regional specialties. The start of Ramadan is also marked by giving to charity, helping those in need, and seeking forgiveness as individuals embrace the spiritual goals of the holy month. It is a time of unity, devotion, and renewal for Filipino Muslims.
