Rizal Day

Rizal Day, observed annually on December 30, honors the life and legacy of Dr. José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. Dr. Rizal was a nationalist, revolutionary, and intellectual who played a crucial role in the country's fight for independence from Spanish colonial rule. His writings, particularly his novels Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo, exposed the injustices of Spanish rule and ignited the Filipino people's desire for freedom. Despite his advocacy for reform rather than violent revolution, Rizal's execution by the Spanish authorities in 1896 became a rallying point for the Philippine Revolution and further galvanized the Filipino quest for independence.

Rizal Day commemorates the martyrdom of Dr. Rizal, who was executed in Bagumbayan (now Luneta Park) in Manila. The day serves as a reminder of his sacrifices for the country, and it is an occasion to reflect on his contributions to Philippine society, including his advocacy for education, reform, and human rights. Rizal's life and death continue to inspire Filipinos to uphold the values of freedom, patriotism, and the pursuit of justice.

What Do People Do?

On Rizal Day, Filipinos observe a variety of activities to honor Dr. Rizal’s memory and contributions to the nation. Flag-raising ceremonies are held at Rizal Park in Manila, where the Rizal Monument stands as a symbol of his sacrifice and legacy. These ceremonies are attended by government officials, students, and citizens, and often include speeches, patriotic songs, and cultural performances that reflect on Rizal’s life and ideals. The Philippine President usually delivers an address during the national commemoration, emphasizing Rizal’s enduring relevance to the country.
