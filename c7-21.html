Ninoy Aquino Day

Ninoy Aquino Day, observed annually on August 21, commemorates the life and legacy of Benigno "Ninoy" Aquino Jr., a prominent Filipino politician and opposition leader during the Marcos dictatorship. Aquino was a vocal critic of Ferdinand Marcos' regime, advocating for democracy, human rights, and political reform. His tragic assassination at the Manila International Airport (now named Ninoy Aquino International Airport in his honor) upon returning to the Philippines in 1983 sparked a wave of outrage and galvanized the Filipino people against the oppressive Marcos government. His death became a turning point in Philippine history, leading to the eventual People Power Revolution in 1986 that ousted Marcos and restored democracy to the country.

Ninoy Aquino is remembered for his unwavering commitment to the Filipino people and his sacrifice for the country's freedom. His death is often seen as a catalyst for change, symbolizing the fight against authoritarianism and the power of civil resistance.

What Do People Do?

On Ninoy Aquino Day, Filipinos observe the occasion through various activities that honor his life and contributions to the nation’s democracy. Ceremonial activities are held in key locations, including at the Ninoy Aquino Monument in Quezon City, where government officials, families, and citizens gather to reflect on Aquino's legacy. The day is also marked by special Masses, public discussions, and documentaries that revisit Aquino's political career, his fight for justice, and the impact of his death on the Philippines' democratic journey.
