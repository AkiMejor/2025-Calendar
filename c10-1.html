All Saints' Day

All Saints' Day, known as Araw ng mga Santo in the Philippines, is celebrated annually on November 1. It is a Christian holiday dedicated to honoring all the saints, both known and unknown, who have attained heaven. The day is meant to celebrate the lives of those who have lived exemplary Christian lives and are recognized for their holiness, virtue, and devotion. The Catholic Church in particular encourages the faithful to remember the saints' sacrifices and their role in interceding for believers.

In the Philippines, All Saints' Day is an important religious and cultural observance. Filipinos honor their loved ones who have passed away, and the day is deeply intertwined with the Filipino tradition of remembering the dead. It is a time for reflection on faith, holiness, and the communion of saints, as well as a reminder of the afterlife and the importance of spiritual growth.

What Do People Do?

On All Saints' Day, Filipinos observe several religious and cultural traditions, starting with attending Mass to honor the saints and remember the departed. Many families visit the graves of their loved ones at cemeteries, where they clean and decorate tombstones with flowers, candles, and other offerings. This practice is a sign of respect and remembrance, and it is also an opportunity for families to bond together. Some Filipinos stay at the cemetery overnight in a tradition called “Pista ng mga Patay”, where they light candles, offer prayers, and reflect on the lives of their deceased relatives.
