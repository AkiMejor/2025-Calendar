Independence Day

Independence Day in the Philippines, celebrated annually on June 12, marks the country’s declaration of independence from Spanish colonial rule in 1898. On this day, Emilio Aguinaldo, the leader of the Philippine revolutionary movement, proclaimed the nation’s independence in Kawit, Cavite. The declaration was made in the presence of fellow revolutionaries, and the Philippine flag was unfurled for the first time. This moment is considered a pivotal point in Philippine history, as it signified the end of more than 300 years of Spanish colonial dominance.

While the declaration of independence occurred in 1898, the Philippines' journey to full sovereignty was prolonged. The country was later ceded to the United States after the Spanish-American War, and it was not until July 4, 1946, that the Philippines officially gained full independence. However, June 12 was chosen as the official date for the observance of Independence Day, as it represents the first clear assertion of the Filipino people’s desire for freedom.

What Do People Do?

On Independence Day, Filipinos commemorate the country’s freedom through various activities, ceremonies, and patriotic displays. Flag-raising ceremonies are held in public spaces, government offices, and schools, with the national anthem, Lupang Hinirang, played to honor the significance of the day. The President of the Philippines typically leads the national commemoration, with speeches that reflect on the nation's struggles for independence, its history, and the importance of preserving freedom and democracy.
